# General
- Unless otherwise specified, you may assume I am using 5e rules for game mechanics
- **Inspiration**: you may have up to 3 stacks. Don't forget about it and please track it, I know it can be difficult. 
# Character creation 
- **Race** - Players handbook, volos, eberron, xanathars, swordcoast (I realize this is restrictive and normally I don't like to do this. In this campaign however I would like to try and stick to mostly standard races in an effort to keep gameplay focused on the story and less on some of the strange race interactions that can happen. There are so many weird race abilities and features that fit well in their setting but for here please humor me. I don't plan on using this restriction for future campaigns). 
- Renaissance firearms allowed (you still need to gain proficiency)
- ***Starting stats: point buy***
- You may use 2014(5e) or 2024(5.5e) when picking a class or feat. I don't care which one you use but please be consistent. If you want a subclass from a different edition that doesn't match up with the standard class progression (ie: a 5.5e cleric with a 5e domain) then talk to me and we will work it out. When picking a 5e feat you may not pick the same feat from 5.5 later and vice versa. When picking spells you may choose from both 5e and 5.5 lists for your class/subclass but you must choose only one edition of each spell to add to your list. 
- 5.5e rules for origin feat, even if you are playing a 5e class (this means at level one you may pick any origin feat).
- Weapon masteries will apply for each class as though you are playing a 5.5e class, even if you are playing a 5e class. 
- **Tool proficiencies**: xanathars guide to everything, chapter 2 page 78-80 has an incredible excerpt on using tool proficiencies that I feel goes criminally underutilized by players and dms. In all of my campaigns since the release of the book I have attempted to maximize the use of this clever section. I highly advise all of you to read it and take advantage of the uses of tool proficiencies that may not be readily evident. Feel free to try and convince me why your proficiency may be applicable in certain situations.
- I don't want to worry about rations or encumbrance rules unless specific situations call for it. This is not to be construed as "please feel free to carry every item in the players handbook in your backpack".
