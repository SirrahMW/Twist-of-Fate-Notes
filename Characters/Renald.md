# Biography
## Early Life
Renald was born to **Arno** and **Nadine Freund** in **Polvora**, the capital city of **Colina**, during the hottest day of the hottest month. His name has no particular meaning but was simply given as a namesake to honor a deceased friend of his father's. Growing up in an impoverished mining town, **Renald**'s family struggled to make ends meet.

**Renald**'s relationship with his father, **Arno**, was built on a foundation of curiosity and inquiry. Despite **Arno**'s busy schedule as a miner, **Renald** would eagerly await his return home from work, bombarding him with questions about various subjects, including herbs, history, and even girls. **Arno**, though often unsure of the answers himself, encouraged **Renald**'s thirst for knowledge by occasionally purchasing books that addressed specific topics. This exchange fostered a strong bond between them, as they would engage in discussions, with **Arno** guiding **Renald** towards finding his own solutions or providing him with resources to explore further.

Renald's mother, **Nadine**, was a kind and compassionate woman who instilled in him a deep appreciation for the natural world. Having grown up on a ranch in the **Plata**, she possessed basic knowledge of medicine, which she applied by stitching wounds and delivering babies without expectation of payment. Her selfless nature earned her the respect and gratitude of their community, as she provided essential services to those in need. **Nadine**'s influence had a profound impact on **Renald**, shaping his values and teaching him the importance of caring for others and the land they inhabited.

Thus these two commoners were able to raise a kind and caring young boy even from the less-than-ideal quality of life they endured. Renald had many friends growing up, and was liked by most. That didn't save him from being seen as a street urchin by passing-through merchants or guild traders, sometimes they would even kick at him and shoo as they pushed passed him in the street. This never once hurt his positive outlook on life though, as his mother or father always had a way of talking him through it. 

>"They're in their own little world sweetie, they didn't do that to *you*, but their *idea* of you. But you know that you're more than an urchin"

Renald's best friends growing up were a wild-haired human boy named Lucas and a more stoic gnome named Rufus. They were a ragtag bunch who got up to harmless mischief often. While harmless, not necessarily always legal.

### The Fall of Arno

Tragedy struck when Renald was just 15 years old. His father, **Arno**, fell ill with one of the first recorded cases of the Creeping Pox, a mysterious and deadly disease that originated from deep under the earth. The primary symptom of the Pox is a slow necrotizing of flesh, causing painful lesions and disfiguring scars.

As the disease spread throughout Colina's mines, Renald was forced to watch his father succumb to its cruel grasp. **Nadine**, Renald's mother, took immediate action to protect her son from the deadly disease. With her knowledge of basic medicine, she recognized the risks associated with the Pox and made the difficult decision to keep Renald isolated from his father.

Renald was heartbroken at being separated from his ailing father, but he understood the gravity of the situation. He spent long days cooped up in their small home, waiting anxiously for news about **Arno**'s condition. Despite **Nadine**'s best efforts, **Arno** never recovered from the Pox.

Renald's life took a dark turn as he struggled to cope with the loss of his father and the harsh realities of their world. The Creeping Pox had already claimed many lives in Colina, leaving behind a trail of devastation and fear. Renald felt lost and alone, forced to grow up too quickly in the face of such tragedy.

#### Coping Mechanisms

Renald's response to his father's illness was to throw himself into work. After all, they couldn't afford for him not to. At just 15 years old, he found employment in a barely-profitable mine, where he labored long hours for meager pay. This grueling schedule took a toll on his physical and mental health, but it also provided him with a sense of purpose.

As Renald grew older, he moved to better-paying mines, but the work remained grueling and often hazardous. He became increasingly withdrawn, struggling to form close relationships or find joy in life. The pain of losing his father still lingered, and Renald felt like he was living in a constant state of grief.

#### Long-term Consequences

The loss of **Arno** had far-reaching consequences for Renald's life. His relationship with his mother, **Nadine**, became strained as she struggled to cope with her own grief. Renald's decision to work in the mines at such a young age also meant that he missed out on opportunities for education and personal growth. He stopped being able to regularly see his friends, and his childhood effectively ended here.

As Renald navigated this difficult period, he began to develop coping mechanisms that would serve him well in the years to come. He learned to rely on himself, to be resourceful and determined in the face of adversity. However, these traits came at a cost, as **Renald** struggled with feelings of isolation and disconnection from others.

## Resurrection

Renald's life was forever changed when he fell ill with the Creeping Pox himself. As he knew it was a death sentence, and infectious, he removed himself from the physical vicinity of everyone he could. He hid in dark corners of the mines, away from others, until either starvation or the pox took him. However, he was resurrected through unknown magic, his body modified to continue working in the mines.

- **The Debt:** Renald is now responsible for paying back the initial lump hiring bonus, which has been increased by the costs of his resurrection and body modifications.
- **The Parasite:** A larva part of a greater whole, Renald's parasite is a unique entity that sometimes communicates with him directly or through the entire entity. His link with the parasite is still weak, but could grow stronger each time he uses it's power.

He was allowed to return to the surface and live a relatively normal life. At least, it would have seemed normal if it weren't for his changed senses. Or for the fact that he tried to tell his mother what happened, searching for support in this strange happenstance, only for that to push her over the edge. She lashed out at him for being a "demon" who replaced her son. This dealt such a blow to Renald's mental state that it was years before he even spoke to someone other than the mine supervisor.

### "Life" as an Arisen

Renald's existence is now a delicate balance between life and death. He struggles to maintain his humanity in the face of his unnatural state. His memories from before his resurrection remain vivid, but he often feels disconnected from the world around him. He makes a daily promise to himself that he won't become the monster he appears to be without his magical glamour. He reminds himself of the values his mother hammered into him; that every single life is valuable. He usually accomplishes this by reciting rhymes or phrases that his mother used to cite back to him when he was a child.

> "If I die again, even for one person to live, it will all be worth it. My life ended a long time ago."

- **The Struggle:** Renald longs to feel again, to experience the simple joys of life like hugging a loved one or feeling the warmth of the sun. ~40 long years as an Arisen has dulled the memory of what each felt like, but he will still occasionally dream about those sensations. The desire for feeling again is really strong, and many Arisen fall into substance abuse easily.
- **The Danger:** If Renald were to die, his family and friends in the Colina would likely pay the price. He is trapped in this existence, forced to continue working in the mines to pay off his debt.

> "A small part of me wants for destruction and death. A new part. A foreign part."

It's difficult to get used to moving around without being able to feel much of anything. No longer feeling the wind on your face or the warmth of the sun. No satisfaction of hugging a loved one. Every touch is like that of a gloved hand, you can tell you are touching something, but feeling fine details is impossible. Dulled emotions are also a struggle. Renald longs to feel again, even for a moment. As do many Arisen, which can be told by the amount that fall into substance abuse.

It's been hard for the 3 or more decades he has been in-between, between death and living. He has to remind himself daily, almost like a prayer, of the value each life holds. Each one is priceless, a treasure to be protected and cherished, unlike his own. From a young age, his mother always taught him to respect others and the land itself, and he was not going to let himself disappoint her, even if she would never know.
